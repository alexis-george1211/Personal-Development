About Me

Personal Aspects

In here you will get to know me more with the use of the eight personal aspects of self development.

Physical Self

I am standing at 5 feet 5 inches and I weigh 40 kilos. I have a black shoulder length hair. I have a round face with big brown eyes and two toned lips. I also have an overbite which explains the process of my braces. Honestly, it's the moment frustrating thing to have. I don't often work out, only when I find the time to, or when I feel like it. I run, but not too much because I have asthma. But I can walk a great distance when partnered with music!

I like to dress myself in layers, pretending like I'm in Dead Poets Society or an old academia, but due to the climate, I try to dress casually. You wouldn't see me usually in a dress but sometimes I take liking in wearing them. But I never liked to wear skirts. How ironic, right?

Intellectual Self

I'm a person who likes to crave for knowledge but only at the expense of my own comfort. I don't like to be forced to learn things I'm not particularly interested in. But, I try my best to participate, especially in school. Which my main struggle is Mathematics. Numbers are my main enemy when it comes to Maths. But sometimes, if I focus hard enough, I would be able to understand it. It's not promised that I would be able to remember it the next day though!

What I love studying most is English or anything that involves Literature. If there's one place I'd be grateful to be stuck with, it's definitely a libary. It always excites me when one person references literary texts, or when it's shown on lectures. I'm a total bookworm! To add to this, I am a huge literature nerd; I love essays, writing poems, analyzing songs, deep conversations, broadway theatres, annotating books, and giving out speeches (if given enough time to practice). I also love doing debates and understanding different sides. It always excites me when I wouldn't be able to get the side I'm aiming for. It's funny to think so. But, with that, it helps me critically think.

But my weakest link would be impromptu speaking since I have a very foggy brain when it comes to forming proper sentences in such limited time, let alone no time at all! I think fast but not in an orderly manner, so it would always hinder my formation of sentences. But, to combat this, I can write what I think despite how fast it is. So, in conclusion, don't put me in a place where I have to think fast while also forming sentences out of my mouth. Especially in front of an audience. But this doesn't apply to my debating skills, though, thanks. If you tell me to think fast, my brain would immediately shut down. So, it's best to say to just leaving me alone to my thoughts and let the pieces fall into place. Kidding!

I also take fun learning about psychology. I like to navigate around individual behaviours to further and better understand why they do things the way they do. It also helps me learn things about myself that I have a hard time understanding as well. Which is why if I had given a third choice in college courses, it would be Psychology.

Speaking of college courses, my first choice would be Medicine. I love learning things about human anatomy and bodily functions. I want to know how to combat diseases and illneses. It helps me stay motivated to learn about biological aspects of human beings because in the end, I want to give people hope.

I also indulge in various hobbies that challenges my way of thinking, which, currently, in coding. I love playing Sudoku, Wordle, Brain Puzzles, and anything in New York Times Magazine that tickles my brain!

All in all, I am confident that I am a fast learner. If I put enough focus on a material, I would be able to learn it by the end of the day.

Emotional Self

In reference to my intellectual self, I have mentioned that I think fast that I wouldn't be able to keep track of it. Easiest way to explain it is, I have a head that's always running in marathons that speaks to me in jumbled things. Good things, bad things, any things. That's why I keep a journal, though inconsistently, I would still be able to make up a thought.

I'm not as open when it comes to asking for help when I understand that a situation has become difficult for me to handle. I've always feared abandonment when people know what I'm going through.

I have always felt like I am a lost person. It's the heavy weight of feeling that I don't belong in places where I am now. I often feel like I simply float around my circle of friends that I constantly find myself constantly staring at a distance. I often find myself detaching from social gatherings to have a time of my own walking, thinking, talking. But, despite that, I remain thankful of my friends for being patient and sticking up to me. Though, I feel most guilty about it, I remain in gratitude for them.

I've always been a distant child, yet I remain open minded to understand that people are experiencing things that way I do. That's why I always fear of accidently putting out my anger on people who try to help me. In my understanding, being helped is a sign of vulnerability, that I have allowed myself to be weak. But, I also know that that isn't how it's supposed to be.

I express my emotios best in writing, music, sketching, or walking paces. I try to keep a healthy mindset that I know it will all work out in the end.

Sensual Self

My eyesight is not my best contender when it comes to seeing the world. I have poor eyesight at a certain distance that may seem visible to others. When I read some words are not what they seem, they're either slanted differently, phrased differently in my head than what was written, sometimes––it's hard to explain it specifically––some words even dance! The medical term for it is dyslexia. I like to challenge it by reading a lot. D espite it, I'm a very observant person--like a wallflower. I tend to look out for social cues that most people might miss.

My hearing wouldn't also be my strong suit, but sometimes my ears can pick up details others don't typically pay attention to. Sort of a selective hearing. But, I also have trouble hearing.

Aside from that, I love mountain views, flower fields, bookcasings, seashores, night skies, sunsets, and sunrises. I love the smell of coffee though I don't drink it. I love the smell of books, flowers, freshly baked bread, toasted nuts, good food. I love listening to music, the sound of waves crashing the shore, the sound of early morning birds, crickets at night. I dislike loud noises, especially city noises, it hurts my head. I prefer the peaceful run of the like, the wind through the tree leaves, and the voices of the people I love.

I don't like crowded places, it makes it harder for me to breathe. I don't like people touching me, especially when I'm not close with them or when they're wet. Though, ironically, I'm touchy with my closest friends. I could say that physical touch is my love language. I love the touch of everyone that I love. I love to hug, cuddle, and dance with people I love most.

Interactional Self

I'm an introverted person who turns into the loudest one when I get close to someone. I'm always open to friendships that I can relate to. I maintain my quiet when I'm not address, though sometimes my opinions can slip through my tongue.

I like to crack a joke, one might say that I have a humour of a 10 year old boy. My friends would call me clingy for always needing to have them near me. I like being close to my love ones. Sometimes overtly close to my bestfriend Atasha that we get mistaken as a couple.

Though, I will always have the tendency to distance myself from social gatherings. My social battery drains fast that I'd go mute for the rest of the day, or excuse myself to be on my own.

I'm quite quiet when it comes to my family, I've always been a distant child. My words would probably not reach a 100 when I'm at home. I tend to stay inside my room and curl up in my bed. I'd bother my brother if I feel like it, but most of the time, I prefer to be on my own.

Nutrional Self

I have a weird diet for myself. I would go for days without eating anything at all. It's because of my weird stomach pains every time I eat. If I eat too much it would hurt, if I eat small amounts it would hurt. That's why I had the most comfort if I don't eat at all. It also affects my heart, I get palpitations by just eating. It became hard to explain to people that cares why I don't eat so I just simply lie to them that I have already eaten or that I feel food.

On the other hands, I like savory foods, especially the ones people consider as "junk foods". Life is short to worry about what you eat! I also like desserts like cheesecake, tiramisu, and ice cream. I don't like overly sweet foods, it tastes bland to me. Just the right amount would do and leave the over consumption to me!

Contextual Self

I tend to adapt depending on where I am and who I’m with, but at the core, I’m still me—just different versions of myself in different situations. In social settings, especially with people I don’t know well, I can be a bit awkward. I sometimes overthink what to say or how to act, but once I get comfortable, my personality starts to show. With friends, it’s a whole different story—I’m fun, energetic, and more outgoing. I feel like I can be my real self without worrying about how I come across. Whether we’re joking around, having deep talks, or just doing random things, I enjoy being in my element with people I trust. With family, I’m not that close, so I don’t really express myself the same way I do with friends. I might be more reserved or just keep things on the surface rather than sharing a lot about myself. It’s not that I don’t care—it’s just that the bond isn’t the same as what I have with my friends. In work or school settings, I try to socialize as much. I might still be a bit awkward, but I try to be responsible and put out an effort to be a leader for my classroom and my obligtions at school. I won’t go out of my way to be the center of attention, but I do my best when it matters.

Spiritual Self

My spiritual self is centered around my personal relationship with God. I see Him as a friend rather than just a figure of religion. It’s not about strict routines or going to church every Sunday—it’s about having a connection with Him in my own way. I talk to God in my thoughts, through prayers that feel more like conversations. I know He’s there, guiding me even when I don’t always have everything figured out. My faith isn’t about following every rule perfectly but about growing, learning, and trusting that He understands me. Even when life feels uncertain, I believe that God has a plan. I don’t always see it right away, but I try to have faith that things will work out in the way they’re meant to. I find comfort in knowing that no matter what happens, I’m never truly alone. My spiritual self isn’t about checking boxes or fitting into a mold—it’s about having a real, personal connection with God, leaning on Him in both the good and the hard times, and striving to be a better person in a way that feels true to me.
